📈 Market Intelligence — Trends & Trade Policy

Policy Australia tariffs on Indian furniture now near-zero (India–Australia ECTA) Jan 2026 · Australia
Under the India–Australia ECTA, furniture tariffs are being phased out (2–6 years) and by Jan 2026 ~100% of Indian goods enter Australia duty-free. Australia is your #1 target market — this is a major pricing advantage over non-FTA suppliers. source
Incentive RoDTEP export rebate returns to full rates from 1 Apr 2026 Apr 2026 · India (all exports)
Furniture exporters get a 1–4% RoDTEP rebate on FOB value. Rates are capped at 50% of notified levels until 31 Mar 2026, then full rates resume — build the higher rebate into Q2 pricing/quotes. source
Trend African furniture market ~$20B and growing fast 2025 · Africa
Urbanisation and a rising middle class (500M+ Africans moving to cities by 2030) are lifting import demand. Largest importers: Nigeria, Morocco, Algeria, Kenya, Egypt, Angola, Ghana. source
Trend South Africa furniture market $2.6B → $3.7B by 2032 2025 · South Africa
Steady growth in your highest-scored African market — worth sustained, consistent outreach. source
Risk Strait of Hormuz effectively closed — Gulf shipping on hold Jun 2026 · Gulf / Middle East
The 2026 Gulf war has cut Hormuz traffic to ~5% of normal. Mundra→Gulf (UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Iraq) is unreliable, so those markets are paused. Saudi via Jeddah (Red Sea) and Oman remain reachable. source
Trend Global furniture imports ~$93B, up 3.5% year-on-year 2024 · Global
Overall world import demand for furniture is rising, supporting continued export expansion. source
